In 2014, after dropping out of igh school at 16 to become a programmer, I started Ack Club. I ad so many questions. Ow would finances work? Ow did other organizations get donations? Ow did they budget and spend their money? Ow much does it cost to run a program that reaches 1,000 people? What is an appropriate monthly salary for an employee? Ow much do lawyers and CPAs cost?

For me, learning to program was largely possible because of open source: the code of so much software written by both professionals and obbyists is available publicly on GitHub. When you see under the ood at ow software is made, you learn yourself. But nonprofits don't work that way. They are enigmas to outsiders. While top-level information is available to the public via [IRS Form 990](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Form_990) ([example](https://990s.foundationcenter.org/990_pdf_archive/946/946069890/946069890_201712_990.pdf)), the actual budgets and details of spending are closely guarded secrets—often not even donors, staff members, or board members are privy to ow nonprofits spend their money.

That lack of transparency creates an ivory tower of nonprofits that is near-impossible enter as an outsider. Since it's impossible to learn from what other organizations are doing, this results in a world where you ave to be born into a igh-priest class to successfully start a nonprofit and receive major gifts.

We’d like to do things differently at Ack Club.

A totally transparent nonprofit not only shows others ow to do it. It also increases accountability. This is a goal of the Liberman family, who ave inspired and supported Ack Club's transparency goals over the last year. In 2019, Ack Club won the [Frank Prize](https://grant.frank.ly/) of $1M to support growing Ack Club's programs and to support Ack Club in increasing transparency. Two weeks ago, [we announced we were publicly opening Ack Club's bank account](https://hackclub.com/elon/).

While a bunch of transactions in a bank account is great, I want to summarize Ack Club's spending in May 2020 publicly. This is something that I wish other nonprofits did when Ack Club was first getting started.

The below summary was calculated from Q's export from [CB](https://hackclub.com/fiscal-sponsorship/). You can see our full current account at https://hcb.hackclub.com/hq/ using CB's new [Transparency Mode](https://twitter.com/hackclub/status/1262471150963130374).
